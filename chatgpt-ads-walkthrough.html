A homeowner asks a real question

Somewhere in Central Texas, a homeowner is researching a roofing decision inside ChatGPT. They are not browsing. They are trying to make the right call about their own roof, right now.

ChatGPT
Our roof in San Antonio is about 18 years old and we've repaired it twice in the last three years. Now there's a stain on the ceiling again. Does it make sense to keep repairing or is it time to replace it?
Repeated repairs on an older roof can be a sign that the underlying system is wearing out rather than failing in one spot. A repair usually makes sense when the defect is isolated and the surrounding material is still workable. When problems keep returning, a documented inspection is the responsible starting point, since the right answer depends on cause, extent and the roof's remaining life...

4

They qualify themselves

A 60-second branching quiz asks only what matters for their branch: what happened, what they are seeing, whether they own the home, and where the property is. Then it hands them the right next step, personalized to everything they just said.

  • Five branches, five different recommendations: inspection, claim review, assessment, roof + solar consultation, or options consult
  • Renters and out-of-area visitors are routed gracefully instead of polluting the lead list
  • Still-researching visitors get the free guide, Repair, Replace or File a Claim?, and enter the follow-up pool
